Solving the Core Robotics Gap & Skilled Labor Shortage

The Industry Constraint (The Problem)

Current robot integration is costly ($30K–$120K average per robot) and slow (2–8 weeks to production). This high barrier to entry is compounded by:

  • Skilled Labor Crisis: Over 52% of integrators still rely on manual programming and proprietary languages, creating a major bottleneck.
  • Legacy Systems: Integrating new robots with existing legacy systems remains a complex, time-consuming, and expensive hindrance to adoption.
  • Reactive Safety: Most collision avoidance is reactive (stopping), not predictive, which limits true Human-Robot Collaboration.
